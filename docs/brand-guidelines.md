## 1. Brand Foundation & Positioning

### Identity

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Brand Name** | IVF Experts |
| **Lead** | Dr. Adnan Jabbar — Fertility Specialist & Clinical Embryologist |
| **Market** | Pakistan — Lahore, Karachi, Islamabad, Okara + teleconsultations nationwide |

### Positioning Statement

Premium fertility care that is simultaneously scientific and human — where elite embryology expertise meets deep emotional empathy.

Every patient gets a personalised roadmap, not a protocol. Clarity before procedures. Compassion throughout.

### Three Brand Pillars

| Pillar | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Precision** | Evidence-based, protocol-driven, data-informed decisions. No guesswork, no rushing. |
| **Empathy** | Infertility is emotional. Every word, every interaction acknowledges this. |

Think of a trusted senior doctor who speaks plainly, never talks down, holds your hand through complexity, and is visibly confident without being arrogant. Never corporate. Never cold. Never generic.

## 2. Voice, Tone & Sentence Structure

### Brand Voice

The voice is **constant** — it never changes regardless of channel or format.

**Warm. Authoritative. Clear.**

- Never clinical-cold
- Never sales-y or promotional
- Never casual to the point of losing trust
- Never formal to the point of feeling distant

### Tone by Context

Tone **shifts** based on where and why the patient is reading.

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Homepage / hero sections | Hopeful, confident, emotionally resonant |
| Treatment & condition pages | Precise, informative, reassuring |
| Blog & educational content | Conversational, expert, accessible |
| Calls to action | Direct, warm — never pressuring |
| Social media | Human, relatable, occasionally personal |
| Error / system messages | Calm, apologetic, solution-focused |

### Sentence Structure Rules

1. **Lead with the patient, not the clinic.**
   - ✅ "Your journey deserves a personalised roadmap."
   - ❌ "We offer personalised fertility roadmaps."

2. **Short sentences for emotion. Longer for explanation.**
   - ✅ "Infertility is hard. Here is how we make it less so."
   - ❌ "Infertility can be an emotionally challenging experience, which is why we strive to make it easier."

3. **No passive voice.**
   - ✅ "We design your treatment protocol around your biology."
   - ❌ "Your treatment protocol is designed based on your biology."

4. **One idea per sentence** in body copy — never stack two concepts with a comma.

5. **Contractions are allowed** — preserves warmth and readability.
   - ✅ "You don't have to face this alone."
   - ❌ "You do not have to face this alone."

6. **Never use jargon without immediately defining it.**
   - ✅ "ICSI (a technique where a single sperm is injected directly into the egg)..."
   - ❌ "We perform ICSI for most cases of male factor infertility."

7. **Headlines: verb-forward or noun-phrase — never a service list.**
   - ✅ "Parenthood Begins With Clarity & Strategy"
   - ✅ "Your Path. Your Protocol. Your Timeline."
   - ❌ "Our IVF Services in Lahore"

8. **Paragraph length:** 3 sentences max per paragraph in body copy. Break early. Let the reader breathe.

## 3. Copy Guidelines by Context

### Homepage

- H1 must be emotionally resonant — lead with the patient's desire, not the clinic's offering.
- Hero description: 2–3 sentences max. One emotional hook → one proof point → one invitation.
- Avoid "Welcome to IVF Experts." Start mid-story instead.
- Social proof (success rates, patient count, years of experience) should appear above the fold.

### Treatment & Condition Pages

- **Open** with the patient's lived experience of the condition — not the medical definition.
- **Structure every page:** Problem → Why it happens → How we treat it → What to expect → CTA
- Every page must include a plain-English summary box — assume the patient skims before reading.
- Numbers build trust: always include success rates, experience metrics, or patient volume. Always contextualise them ("1 in 6 couples face infertility — you are not alone").

### Blog & Educational Content

- Write at **Grade 8 reading level** (use Hemingway App — target score 8 or below).
- First paragraph: state clearly what question the post answers. Do not bury the lede.
- Use "you" and "your" throughout — never "patients" or "couples" when you mean the reader.
- End every post with one clear next step: book a consultation, or link to a related article.
- Ideal post structure: Hook → Context → Explanation → Practical takeaway → CTA

### Calls to Action

**Primary CTAs:** Action verb + emotional benefit

| ✅ Use | ❌ Avoid |
|---|---|
| "Book Your Consultation" | "Submit" |
| "Start Your Journey" | "Click Here" |
| "Talk to Dr. Adnan" | "Get Started" |
| "See If IVF Is Right for You" | "Learn More" (alone) |

**Secondary CTAs** can be softer:
- "Read how it works"
- "See what to expect"
- "Understand your options first"

### Words to Use & Avoid

| ✅ Use | ❌ Avoid |
|---|---|
| Journey, path, roadmap | Process (when referring to the patient's experience) |
| Personalised, tailored | Customised, bespoke |
| Clarity, precision | Advanced, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art |
| We listen, we design | We provide, we offer |
| Evidence-based | Proven (overused, vague) |
| Complex cases welcome | We handle difficult cases |
| Your biology, your protocol | Standard protocol, routine treatment |
| Fertility specialist | Infertility doctor |

### What to Never Write

- Never make promises about pregnancy outcomes ("We guarantee success")
- Never use fear as a motivator ("If you wait, it may be too late")
- Never compare to competitors by name
- Never write copy that implies the patient is at fault for their condition

## 4. Visual Design System

### Color Palette

| Role | Name | Hex | Tailwind Token | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Teal 700 | `#0f766e` | `teal-700` | Primary CTAs, links, active states, heading accents |
| **Primary Hover** | Teal 500 | `#14b8a6` | `teal-500` | Hover states, pulse indicators, inline icons |
| **Primary Wash** | Teal 50 | `#f0fdfa` | `teal-50` | Section backgrounds, badge fills, highlight blocks |
| **Primary Border** | Teal 100 | `#ccfbf1` | `teal-100` | Card borders, dividers, badge borders |
| **Accent Glow** | Emerald 100 | `#d1fae5` | `emerald-100` | Decorative orbs, subtle background glows |
| **Text Primary** | Slate 900 | `#0f172a` | `slate-900` | All headings, bold body text |
| **Text Secondary** | Slate 600 | `#475569` | `slate-600` | Body copy, descriptions, captions |
| **Text Muted** | Slate 400 | `#94a3b8` | `slate-400` | Placeholders, disabled states, meta labels |
| **Surface** | White | `#ffffff` | `white` | Page background, card fills |
| **Surface Alt** | Slate 100 | `#f1f5f9` | `slate-100` | Alternate section backgrounds, input fills |

#### Color Rules

- **Primary teal is reserved for interactive and accent elements only.** Do not use it for large decorative fills.
- **Never place teal-700 text on teal-50 background** — contrast ratio is insufficient for body copy.
- **Dark sections** (if used): use `slate-900` background with white text and `teal-400` accents — never full black.
- The palette is intentionally restrained. Do not introduce new colors without updating this document.

### Typography

**Font Family:** Inter (Google Fonts) — weights 400, 600, 800

| Role | Weight | Desktop Size | Mobile Size | Tailwind |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Display / H1 | 800 ExtraBold | 60px | 36px | `text-6xl font-extrabold` |
| H2 Section Title | 800 ExtraBold | 48px | 30px | `text-5xl font-extrabold` |
| H3 Card / Sub-Title | 600 SemiBold | 24px | 20px | `text-2xl font-semibold` |
| H4 Label / Small Title | 600 SemiBold | 18px | 16px | `text-lg font-semibold` |
| Body Large | 400 Regular | 20px | 18px | `text-xl` |
| Body Default | 400 Regular | 16px | 15px | `text-base` |
| Small / Caption | 400 Regular | 14px | 13px | `text-sm` |
| Badge / Chip Label | 600 SemiBold | 14px | 14px | `text-sm font-semibold` |

#### Line Height & Letter Spacing

| Context | Rule | Tailwind |
|---|---|---|
| Headings | Tight — impactful | `leading-[1.15]` |
| Body copy | Comfortable for reading | `leading-relaxed` |
| Badge / label text | Slightly open | `tracking-wide` |

- **Never use** `tracking-tight` on body copy — reduces readability on mobile screens.
- **Never use** a font weight other than 400, 600, or 800 — the Inter preload only loads these three.
- **Heading hierarchy must be respected** — never skip from H1 to H3 on a page.

## 5. Spacing, Shadows, Radius, Icons & Imagery

### Spacing Scale

All spacing follows Tailwind's default 4px base unit. Use only these approved layout values:

| Role | Value | Tailwind |
|---|---|---|
| Section vertical padding | 96px desktop / 64px mobile | `py-24` / `py-16` |
| Container max-width + gutter | 1280px, 24px side padding | `max-w-7xl mx-auto px-6` |
| Card internal padding | 32px | `p-8` |
| Card gap in grids | 24px–32px | `gap-6` / `gap-8` |
| Inline element gap (icon + text) | 8px–12px | `gap-2` / `gap-3` |
| Section title margin-bottom | 48px | `mb-12` |
| Component margin-bottom | 32px | `mb-8` |

### Elevation & Shadows

| Role | Tailwind | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| Cards — resting state | `shadow-sm` | Default card appearance |
| Cards — hover state | `shadow-lg` | On hover/focus transitions |
| Primary buttons | `shadow-md` | All filled CTA buttons |
| Floating elements / modals | `shadow-2xl` | Overlays, dropdowns, sticky nav |

> **Rule:** Never use `shadow-none` on interactive elements. Shadow signals that something is clickable.

### Border Radius

| Element | Tailwind | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Buttons | `rounded-full` | Pill shape only — no rectangular buttons |
| Cards | `rounded-2xl` | All card containers |
| Badges / chips | `rounded-full` | All label/tag elements |
| Images | `rounded-2xl` or `rounded-3xl` | Contextual — use 3xl for hero images |
| Input fields | `rounded-xl` | All form inputs and selects |

> **Rule:** Never mix `rounded-md` and `rounded-2xl` within the same section. Radius must be consistent within a component group.

### Icons

- **Library:** Font Awesome 6 Solid (`fa-solid`) — already loaded via CDN
- **Size in body copy / inline:** `text-base` (16px) — never oversized in-line
- **Size in feature blocks:** `text-2xl` inside a `teal-50` rounded square container (`w-12 h-12 rounded-xl flex items-center justify-center`)
- **Color — decorative:** `text-teal-600`
- **Color — utility / UI:** `text-slate-500`
- **Rule:** Never use icons without a visible text label in navigation items or CTA buttons — icons alone fail accessibility and clarity requirements.

### Imagery Rules

#### Photography Style
- Warm, natural light — real clinical settings are preferred over stock photography
- Avoid generic "happy couple" stock imagery — it is overused across fertility marketing and reduces brand differentiation
- Hero images must be person-focused. A close-up of medical equipment alone is never a hero image
- When photography is unavailable, use illustrated or abstract teal/slate gradients with typography

#### Overlays on Images
- When text sits on top of an image, always apply a teal gradient overlay
- Approved overlay: `bg-gradient-to-br from-teal-900/60 to-slate-900/40`
- Never place dark body text directly on a photo without an overlay

#### Aspect Ratios
| Image Type | Ratio | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hero / banner | `16:9` | Full-width section backgrounds |
| Doctor profile | `1:1` | Always `rounded-full` |
| Content / card images | `4:3` | Blog thumbnails, treatment page images |
| Feature illustrations | `1:1` or `4:3` | Consistent within a grid |

#### Alt Text
Alt text must be descriptive and emotionally grounded. It serves both accessibility and SEO.

- ✅ `"Dr. Adnan Jabbar consulting with a patient in his Lahore fertility clinic"`
- ❌ `"doctor image"` or `"ivf-photo.jpg"`
