# Patient Journey Map — ivfexperts.pk
**Assessed:** 2026-03-03 | **Status:** 🔴 Not Started
**Goal:** Map every stage of the patient's path from first symptom to becoming an advocate, identify gaps in IVF Experts' current touchpoints, and define the ideal experience at every stage

---

## Executive Summary

Every other strategy document in this folder optimises a single piece of the patient experience: SEO captures awareness traffic, CRO converts visitors, WhatsApp nurtures leads, the patient portal supports treatment. But no document has mapped how all these pieces connect — or exposed the gaps between them where patients fall through.

In Pakistan's fertility market, the average couple researches for 3–6 months before making first contact with a clinic. The emotional weight is high, the information environment is confusing, and trust is earned slowly. A patient who discovers IVF Experts via a blog post in March may not book until August — but only if there is a coherent nurture path keeping Dr. Adnan's name present and trusted throughout that window.

The journey has 8 stages: Awareness, Research, Consideration, First Contact, Consultation, Treatment, Outcome, and Advocacy. IVF Experts currently has good coverage at Awareness (SEO) and patchy coverage at Research and Consideration. First Contact and Consultation are largely dependent on individual clinic interactions with no documented standard. Treatment and Outcome are supported by the patient portal but incompletely. Advocacy — the referral engine — is entirely undocumented.

**The biggest gap:** There is almost no nurture content or system between Research and First Contact — where the patient is interested but not yet ready to reach out. This 2–4 month window is where most leads are lost.

## PATIENT PERSONAS

### Persona 1 — The Lahori Couple (Primary)
- **Demographics:** Married 2–4 years, wife 28–35, Lahore-based, middle to upper-middle class, dual income
- **Who researches:** Wife leads research; husband involved in decisions
- **Language:** Urdu-dominant, reads English content for medical information
- **Emotional state:** Hopeful but anxious; family pressure is present; IVF carries some stigma
- **Research behaviour:** Google searches in English ("IVF specialist Lahore"), Marham.pk, WhatsApp groups, friends' recommendations
- **Key fears:** Cost, multiple failed cycles, social stigma around IVF
- **Content that reaches them:** Educational blog posts, "what to expect from IVF" content, local success stories
- **Conversion trigger:** A trusted recommendation from a friend or family member + online evidence of Dr. Adnan's credentials

### Persona 2 — The Diaspora Patient
- **Demographics:** Pakistani living in UK, UAE, Canada, or USA; aged 30–42
- **Who researches:** Both partners — often the husband is driving the return trip
- **Language:** English-dominant
- **Emotional state:** Frustrated by cost in their country; hopeful about Pakistan option; needs very high trust to book before visiting
- **Research behaviour:** Deep online research, reads everything, watches YouTube, checks Google reviews meticulously
- **Key fears:** "Is this doctor really qualified?", "Will my information be private?", "What if something goes wrong?"
- **Content that reaches them:** Detailed credential pages, video content from Dr. Adnan, transparent pricing, clear teleconsultation process
- **Conversion trigger:** A teleconsultation that demonstrates Dr. Adnan's competence before committing to travel

### Persona 3 — The Male Factor Patient
- **Demographics:** Man aged 28–45 who has been diagnosed with or suspects azoospermia, low sperm count, or poor morphology
- **Who researches:** He researches alone, privately — rarely discusses with family initially
- **Language:** English and Urdu
- **Emotional state:** Embarrassed, private, often in denial. Infertility stigma for men in Pakistan is severe.
- **Research behaviour:** Highly specific searches ("azoospermia treatment Lahore", "sperm retrieval Pakistan"), reads thoroughly before contact
- **Key fears:** Being judged; the condition being permanent; cost of TESA/microTESE
- **Content that reaches them:** Male infertility pages that treat the patient with dignity, specific condition pages, "you are not alone" framing
- **Conversion trigger:** Content that normalises male factor infertility + evidence that Dr. Adnan has treated this successfully

### Persona 4 — The Repeat Failure Patient
- **Demographics:** Woman aged 32–42 who has had 2+ failed IVF cycles at another clinic
- **Who researches:** She researches extensively — she is now an informed patient and a sceptic
- **Emotional state:** Depleted, sceptical, but still hopeful. Wary of promises.
- **Research behaviour:** Looks for evidence of clinical thinking, not just marketing. Reads about protocols. Wants to understand why past cycles failed.
- **Key fears:** "What will this doctor do differently?", "Am I wasting more money and heartbreak?"
- **Content that reaches them:** Content about complex cases, repeat failure, second opinion content ("Why Your Last IVF Failed"), Dr. Adnan's embryology background as differentiator
- **Conversion trigger:** Content that directly addresses repeat failure and explains what a more thorough approach looks like

## STAGE 1 — AWARENESS

Patient realizes they may have a fertility issue. Google is the primary discovery channel.

### Current State
- Strong treatment and condition pages (35+ pages indexed)
- Blog is sparse — few posts targeting early-stage awareness keywords
- No social media presence to capture passive discovery

### Key Awareness Keywords (currently underserved)
- "signs of infertility in women" / "علامات بانجھ پن"
- "when to see a fertility doctor"
- "how long does it take to get pregnant normally"
- "irregular periods and pregnancy"
- "low sperm count symptoms"
- "why am I not getting pregnant after 1 year"

### ✅ Task 1: Create awareness-stage blog content
- **Problem:** The site captures research-phase traffic (condition-specific) but misses the awareness phase (patients who don't yet know what's wrong)
- **Target content:** 6 blog posts targeting early-stage keywords — see `docs/07-content-strategy.md` for topic list
- **Each post must end with:** "If you have been trying to conceive for 12 months without success, it may be time to speak with a specialist. Dr. Adnan offers both in-person and teleconsultations across Pakistan."
- **Priority:** 🔴 High

## STAGE 2 — RESEARCH

Patient actively researches their specific condition and treatment options. This is the longest stage — can last 1–6 months.

### Current State
- Condition pages are strong (PCOS, azoospermia, IVF, ICSI, etc.)
- No email capture — patients who visit and leave are permanently lost
- No lead magnet or nurture sequence — see `docs/12-whatsapp-email-marketing.md`
- No FAQ content on condition pages
- Blog posts do not link to each other (no content cluster structure)

### ✅ Task 2: Add email capture and lead magnet
- **Problem:** A patient visits `/art-procedures/ivf.php`, reads thoroughly, but is not ready to book. They leave — and IVF Experts has no way to stay in their awareness for the next 3 months.
- **Solution:** A simple lead magnet: "Download Our Free IVF Guide for Pakistani Couples" (PDF, 10–15 pages covering what to expect, how to prepare, key questions to ask your doctor)
- **Gate it behind:** First name + email/WhatsApp number
- **Follow-up:** A 4-week educational email/WhatsApp sequence — see `docs/12-whatsapp-email-marketing.md`
- **Priority:** 🔴 High

### ✅ Task 3: Add FAQ schema to all condition pages
- **Why:** Patients in research phase ask very specific questions. FAQ schema captures featured snippets in Google.
- **Each treatment/condition page needs:** 5–8 FAQs at the bottom, marked up with FAQPage JSON-LD
- **See:** `docs/03-schema-markup.md` for implementation
- **Priority:** 🔴 High

## STAGE 3 — CONSIDERATION

Patient compares specific doctors and clinics. This is where trust is won or lost.

### Current State
- About page exists but does not specifically address "why Dr. Adnan vs. other options"
- No testimonials or patient stories visible
- No pricing transparency — patients looking for cost guidance bounce
- No comparison content ("why embryologist-trained doctors achieve different results")
- Reviews are not visible on the site

### ✅ Task 4: Build a "Why Dr. Adnan" authority page
- **URL:** `/about/why-dr-adnan` or enhance the existing `/about/`
- **Content:**
  - The dual-training differentiator (only clinician + embryologist in the region)
  - Credentials in full
  - A specific statement on complex case experience
  - Anonymized patient outcomes data (success rate by age bracket if available)
  - Direct comparison: what a doctor who is only a clinician sees vs. what Dr. Adnan sees as both
- **Priority:** 🔴 High

### ✅ Task 5: Add patient success stories section
- **See:** `docs/15-reputation-management.md` for ethical guidelines and format
- **Placement:** Homepage + dedicated `/patient-stories` page
- **Priority:** 🔴 High

## STAGE 4 — FIRST CONTACT

Patient reaches out via WhatsApp or contact form. This is a high-anxiety moment — they are sharing a deeply private situation with a stranger.

### Current State
- WhatsApp CTA exists (`wa.me/923111101483`) but has no pre-filled message context
- Contact form exists (`/contact/`) but form copy is generic
- No defined response time standard documented
- No acknowledgement message template for new inquiries

### ✅ Task 6: Add pre-filled WhatsApp context to all CTAs
- **Problem:** A patient clicking a bare WhatsApp link has to write everything from scratch — this adds friction
- **Solution:** Pre-fill the WhatsApp message with context: `wa.me/923111101483?text=Hi%2C+I'd+like+to+enquire+about+a+fertility+consultation+with+Dr.+Adnan.`
- **Implementation:** Update all WhatsApp CTA links across the site to use pre-filled text
- **Priority:** 🔴 High

### ✅ Task 7: Create WhatsApp inquiry response templates
- **Goal:** Every new inquiry receives a warm, informative, response within 2 hours during clinic hours
- **Template — initial response:**
  ```
  السلام علیکم! 🌿 Thank you for reaching out to IVF Experts.

  Dr. Adnan Jabbar reviews every new case personally. To help us understand your situation,
  could you briefly share:

  1. How long have you been trying to conceive?
  2. Have you had any tests or previous treatments?
  3. Are you based in Lahore, or would you prefer a teleconsultation?

  We'll get back to you with the best next step — usually a consultation recommendation.
  ```
- **Priority:** 🔴 High

## STAGE 5 — CONSULTATION

First appointment — in-person or teleconsultation. The emotional stakes are at their highest.

### ✅ Task 8: Create a pre-consultation information pack
- **What it is:** A WhatsApp message or email sent 24 hours before the consultation with:
  - What to bring (previous test results, medical history summary)
  - What to expect (duration, structure of the appointment)
  - A brief intro paragraph about Dr. Adnan's approach ("We listen first. We run tests second. We never rush into procedures.")
  - Clinic address and parking guidance (for in-person)
- **Delivery:** WhatsApp or email, 24 hours before appointment
- **Priority:** 🟡 Medium

### ✅ Task 9: Define teleconsultation standard and patient instructions
- **Problem:** No documented process for what a teleconsultation at IVF Experts looks like
- **Define:** Platform (WhatsApp video, Zoom, Google Meet?), duration (45–60 min first consult), what the patient should prepare, how the report/recommendation is delivered afterwards
- **Post-teleconsultation:** Send a written summary of Dr. Adnan's recommendations to the patient within 24 hours
- **Priority:** 🟡 Medium

## STAGE 6 — TREATMENT

Active treatment cycle. Patient is in daily/weekly contact with the clinic.

### ✅ Task 10: Map the patient portal against the treatment journey
- **Current state:** Patient portal exists at `/portal/` but its full functionality is undocumented
- **Steps:**
  1. Document what the portal currently offers (lab results, reports, prescriptions)
  2. Map against patient needs during treatment (cycle monitoring updates, medication schedule, next appointment reminders)
  3. Identify gaps — what do patients currently ask via WhatsApp that the portal could answer?
  4. Prioritise 3 improvements to the portal based on the most common patient queries
- **Priority:** 🟡 Medium

### ✅ Task 11: Create mid-treatment check-in messages
- **What:** A WhatsApp check-in sent at key moments during a treatment cycle (after stimulation starts, day before egg retrieval, after embryo transfer)
- **Purpose:** Reduces patient anxiety, builds trust, reduces inbound WhatsApp queries from patients who feel in the dark
- **Example (post-transfer message):**
  ```
  Thinking of you today 🌿 Your embryo transfer was a success — now it's time to rest and let nature do its part.

  Your next step is your beta HCG blood test on [date]. We'll call you as soon as we have the results.

  If you have any questions or concerns before then, please message us anytime.
  ```
- **Priority:** 🟡 Medium

## STAGE 7 — OUTCOME

Pregnancy confirmed or cycle unsuccessful. Both outcomes require a deliberate response.

### ✅ Task 12: Create post-outcome response system
- **Successful outcome:**
  - Personal congratulations message from Dr. Adnan's number (or named team member)
  - Referral to an OB-GYN (if IVF Experts does not provide antenatal care) — this relationship cements long-term trust and referrals
  - Gentle review request (see `docs/15-reputation-management.md` — timing: 4–6 weeks after positive beta HCG)
- **Unsuccessful outcome:**
  - Compassionate, personal message — never templated-sounding
  - Clear explanation of what happens next (rest period, cycle review, protocol adjustment)
  - Optional: a follow-up call from a nurse coordinator after 48 hours
  - Do NOT ask for a review
- **Priority:** 🔴 High

---

## STAGE 8 — ADVOCACY

Successful patient becomes a source of referrals, reviews, and word-of-mouth.

### ✅ Task 13: Build a structured advocacy programme
- **What Pakistani patients do naturally:** Tell friends and family in WhatsApp groups; recommend their doctor to relatives in their home city; post about their experience on Facebook or Instagram (often privately)
- **What IVF Experts should do:**
  1. Systematically ask for referrals 8–12 weeks after a successful pregnancy confirmation
  2. Ask for a Google and Marham review (see `docs/15-reputation-management.md`)
  3. Offer to do an anonymized case study on the blog (with consent) — this gives the patient a story to share
  4. Send annual Eid messages to past patients — keeps the relationship warm for future siblings
- **Referral incentive:** In Pakistan's medical context, financial referral incentives are inappropriate and ethically problematic. Instead: a personal thank-you from Dr. Adnan, a handwritten card, or a small gift (dates/sweets) is culturally resonant and ethical.
- **Priority:** 🟡 Medium
